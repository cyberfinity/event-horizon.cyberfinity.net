Meeting 4

Date: Wednesday, 5. February 2003

Location: Cholo, Sudents' Union South

Minutes taken by: Tom

Present SpaceMonkey(s): Gideon, Alex, Tom, Ben, Mike & James

Absent SpaceMonkey(s): none

Next meeting scheduled for: 1st March, 2003, 26 North Villiers Street.

  • Clarification of what needs to be done
  • Allocation of tasks
  • Setting deadlines

Clarification of what needs to be done

Now that part A of the assignment had been handed it was necessary to review the requirements for part B so that we could begin planning who would do what and when. It was noted that the tasks for part B lended themsleves better to being split among members of the group, unlike part A where some things required all members to work on the same thing.
3 main areas of work were identified:

  1. Java Programming
  2. UML documentation
  3. Misc. documents (eg: Management Report)

Most of the work would be in parts 1 & 2 so we decided to make them top priority for the time being.

It was also decided that a new timetable should be drawn up once the tasks had been allocated to the SpaceMonkeys.

Allocation of tasks

Ben and Gideon were the least confident with their programming abilities and most interested in the UML design, so they were an easy choice for section 1. No one had any objections, so Ben and Gideon were pronounced official SpaceMonkey UML Gurus!

That left the rest for programming. It was decided that the classes that make up our system could be divided into two groups: the internal classes that perform queries and the GUI classes that provide the user interface.
Both Mike and Tom had an interest in Discrete Maths (which it was decided would be useful when implementing the Network class) and they volunteered to take care of the internal classes. Again there were no objections so Mike and Tom became the SpaceMonkey internal Java Class Ninjas.
That left Alex and James to do the GUI side. Fortunately both are interested in user interfaces and so they happily accepted the position of SpaceMonkey GUI Overlords!

Setting Deadlines

Next the order in which tasks should be done were decided. It was decided the UML should come first since it is intended as a blueprint for our system. However it was a agreed upon that some of the smaller and simpler Java classes could be coded almost right away since all information needed to make them was covered in our Part A UML documents.
We decided that once the necessary UML diagrams were ready, the internal and GUI classes could be started on at more or less the same time. Until that point in time it was decided that Mike, Tom, Alex and James should make themselves familiar with the relevant Java knowledge to make their classes. It was also decided that some time should be allocated to testing the system once it is complete.
James was assigned the task of creating a new timetable for the coming weeks to illustrate during what time periods the tasks were to be done and when they should be completed.
